# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)  
*CheckTax — 2026 Nigerian Tax Reform Guide*

---

## What is CheckTax?
CheckTax is a citizen-first tool that explains Nigeria’s 2026 tax reforms in plain language.

It helps people understand:
- What changed
- Whether the reforms apply to them
- What they should be aware of
- Where to go next

It does **not** file taxes or replace government systems.

---

## Is CheckTax an official Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS) platform?
No.

CheckTax is an independent explainer tool.  
It is not owned, run, or endorsed by the Nigeria Revenue Service.

For official filings, disputes, or enforcement matters, the NRS remains the authority.

---

## Who is CheckTax for?
CheckTax is built for:
- Individual Nigerian taxpayers
- Salaried workers and professionals
- Small business owners and startups
- Large corporations
- Lawyers and tax advisors
- Digital asset holders
- Property owners

Different users see different information based on what applies to them.

---

## What tax laws does CheckTax cover?
CheckTax focuses on Nigeria’s **2026 tax reforms**, including:
- New personal income tax bands
- Tax-free income thresholds
- Rent relief and deductions
- Small business exemptions
- Corporate tax rules
- Digital asset taxation
- Property-related tax rules
- Consolidated levies

It does not cover older tax regimes or unrelated laws.

---

## If I earn less than ₦800,000 a year, do I pay tax?
No.

Under the 2026 reforms, income up to **₦800,000 per year is tax-free**.

If your total income falls below this threshold, personal income tax does not apply.

---

## Are small businesses really exempt from multiple taxes?
Yes, if they qualify.

Businesses with **annual turnover below ₦100 million** are exempt from:
- Corporate Income Tax (CIT)
- VAT
- Development levy

This is meant to reduce compliance pressure on small businesses.

---

## Does CheckTax calculate my exact tax bill?
No.

CheckTax shows:
- Applicable tax bands
- High-level impact
- Possible deductions and exemptions

Final tax calculations depend on official filings and assessments.

---

## What about crypto and digital assets?
Gains from digital assets **may be taxable** under the reforms.

Tax applies when profit is made, not simply because an asset is held.  
The exact treatment depends on how and when gains are realized.

For official interpretation, refer to the Nigeria Revenue Service.

---

## Is rent relief automatic?
No.

Rent relief:
- Is capped at **20%**
- With a maximum of **₦500,000 per year**
- Applies only if conditions are met and documented

Not all rent qualifies automatically.

---

## Can I rely on CheckTax for legal or professional advice?
No.

CheckTax explains the law as written.  
It does not provide legal opinions, tax planning strategies, or dispute advice.

If your situation involves:
- Audits
- Disputes
- Penalties
- Appeals
- Complex structures

You should consult the Nigeria Revenue Service or a qualified professional.

---

## Why does CheckTax sometimes refer me to the NRS website?
Some matters can only be handled by the Nigeria Revenue Service, such as:
- Filing procedures
- Official interpretations
- Enforcement actions
- Regulatory updates

When this happens, CheckTax will clearly point you to the NRS website instead of guessing or giving unreliable answers.

This is intentional.

---

## Does CheckTax store my data?
No.

At the MVP stage:
- No accounts are required
- No financial documents are uploaded
- Inputs are session-based only

CheckTax is designed to minimize data collection.

---

## Can I download or save information from CheckTax?
Yes.

Users can download a **PDF version** of relevant explanations for reference purposes.  
This is for personal understanding, not official submission.

CheckTax reflects the reform framework as currently understood.

Tax laws and interpretations can change.  
For the most up-to-date and official guidance, always refer to the Nigeria Revenue Service.
